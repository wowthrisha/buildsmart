## Floor Area Ratio and Ground Coverage — TNCDBR 2019, Rule 9, Tables 2 & 3

### Floor Area Ratio (FAR) / Floor Space Index (FSI)

Floor Area Ratio is the ratio of the total built-up floor area of all floors to the total plot area. FAR and FSI are the same measurement expressed as a ratio.

FAR for Residential R1 zone: 1.5. This means for a 200 sq.m plot, maximum total built-up area across all floors is 300 sq.m.

FAR for Residential R2 zone: 1.75. For a 200 sq.m plot the maximum built-up area is 350 sq.m.

FAR for Commercial C1 zone: 2.0. For a 200 sq.m plot the maximum built-up area is 400 sq.m.

The FAR calculation includes all floors including ground floor, first floor, second floor, and any mezzanine floors. Staircase rooms, lift rooms, and overhead water tanks are excluded from FAR calculation if they do not exceed the permitted area limits.

Exceeding the permitted FAR is an unauthorised construction. The excess built-up area must be demolished or the plan must be revised to bring the FAR within the permitted limit before approval.

To calculate FAR: divide total proposed built-up area by plot area. If the resulting number exceeds the zone limit, reduce built-up area accordingly.

Source: TNCDBR 2019, Rule 9, Table 2. G.O.Ms.No.18, MA&WS, 04-02-2019.

### Ground Coverage

Ground coverage is the ratio of the ground floor footprint area to the total plot area, expressed as a percentage.

Maximum ground coverage for residential buildings: 65 percent of plot area. A 150 sq.m plot may have a maximum ground floor footprint of 97.5 sq.m.

Maximum ground coverage for commercial buildings: 60 percent of plot area.

Ground coverage applies only to the ground floor footprint. Upper floors are governed by FAR, not coverage.

Staircase, lift wells, and covered parking within the building envelope are included in the ground coverage calculation.

Source: TNCDBR 2019, Rule 9, Table 3. G.O.Ms.No.18, MA&WS, 04-02-2019.
