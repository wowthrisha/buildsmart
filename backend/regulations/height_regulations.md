## Height Regulations — TNCDBR 2019 (Amended March 2024)

### Non-High-Rise Building Height Limit

The maximum permitted height for non-high-rise buildings in Tamil Nadu is 14.0 metres, measured from the average ground level to the top of the parapet wall or roof slab.

This 14 metre height limit was amended by G.O.Ms.No.70, Housing and Urban Development, dated 11-03-2024. This supersedes the earlier limit specified in the base TNCDBR 2019 rules.

A building with proposed height of 14 metres or below is classified as a non-high-rise building and requires standard CCMC approval.

### High-Rise Buildings

Any building exceeding 14 metres in height is classified as a high-rise building and requires additional clearances beyond CCMC approval.

High-rise buildings require:
1. Tamil Nadu Fire and Rescue Services NOC
2. Airport Authority of India NOC if within flight path zones
3. Structural stability certificate from a licensed structural engineer
4. Additional setbacks as per high-rise building norms

The approval process for high-rise buildings takes significantly longer than standard residential approvals. High-rise applications go through a separate scrutiny committee.

### Floor-to-Floor Heights

Standard floor-to-floor height for residential buildings is typically 3.0m to 3.5m including slab thickness. For a G+2 building (ground plus 2 upper floors with terrace) the typical height is 9m to 10.5m, well within the 14m limit.

A G+3 building may approach the 14m limit depending on floor heights. Owners planning 4 or more floors including stilt parking should carefully calculate the total height to ensure compliance with the 14 metre limit.

Source: TNCDBR 2019, Rule 9, Height Regulations. G.O.Ms.No.70, Housing and Urban Development, 11-03-2024.
